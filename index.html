About the Conference

FASMI Biennial Meeting 2026

An international, Asia-focused molecular imaging conference bringing together global experts, innovators, clinicians, researchers, industry leaders, science administrators and technology providers. Hosted in Hyderabad, India, the conference serves as a strategic platform to showcase cutting-edge imaging technologies, foster collaboration, and accelerate innovation across Asia and beyond.

Federation of Asian Societies for Molecular Imaging (FASMI)

The Federation of Asian Societies for Molecular Imaging (FASMI) is a collaborative organization uniting molecular imaging societies across the Asia-Pacific region. It was established in September 2006 as a joint initiative of the founding national societies from Japan, Korea, and Taiwan.

FASMI aims to advance the field of molecular imaging by fostering communication, collaboration, and education among researchers, clinicians, and scientists. The society actively advocates for the representation and visibility of Asian molecular imaging communities on the global stage, particularly within the World Molecular Imaging Society (WMIS).

FASMI holds a biennial meeting that serves as a key platform for scientific exchange and promotes collaborative research and innovation within the region. With active participation from societies in Japan, Korea, Taiwan, India, Australia, and others, FASMI continues to support interdisciplinary research, drive technological advancement, and strengthen the collective voice of Asia in the global molecular imaging community.

Molecular Imaging Society of India (MISI)

The Molecular Imaging Society of India (MISI) is a vibrant organization dedicated to advancing multi-modality molecular imaging across the Indian subcontinent. It supports both basic and clinical research, uniting professionals—including clinicians, scientists, engineers, and industry leaders—on a single platform.

Officially inaugurated in 2014, MISI serves as the Indian affiliate of the World Molecular Imaging Society (WMIS). Through collaborations with the Indo American Society of Nuclear Medicine (IASNM) and other leading organizations and universities, MISI fosters knowledge exchange and is committed to enhancing the global presence of Indian molecular imaging research.

The society actively works to translate cutting-edge imaging technologies into meaningful healthcare outcomes. MISI is also deeply invested in supporting students and young researchers, offering opportunities to engage with global experts through its monthly webinar series. As a testament to its commitment to regional collaboration, MISI is honoured to be hosting the upcoming biennial FASMI meeting, at Hyderabad, India.

Osmania University

Osmania University's Central Facilities for Research & Development (CFRD) hosts a Central Instrumentation Laboratory with confocal microscopy, HPLC, AKTA and related platforms well suited for probe validation and molecular-scale imaging.

The university's research centres — notably the Centre for Plant Molecular Biology and the Institute of Genetics and Hospital for Genetic Diseases — provide molecular-biology expertise, training and preclinical models that support tracer development and translational research.

Close links with Osmania Medical College and nearby clinical imaging services facilitate multidisciplinary collaboration, practical workshops and early clinical translation (PET/SPECT) of imaging agents and protocols.
