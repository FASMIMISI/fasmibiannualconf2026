Speakers

Meet the world-class experts shaping the future of molecular imaging.

Keynote Speakers

Dr. Ramasamy Paulmurugan

Professor of Radiology, Stanford University

Dr. Ramasamy Paulmurugan is a core faculty member of the Molecular Imaging Program at Stanford (MIPS). A pioneer in split reporter protein complementation technologies, his work has significantly advanced real time visualization of cellular signaling in living systems.

He leads the Cellular Pathway Imaging Laboratory (CPIL), where his research focuses on multimodality in vivo imaging, gene and microRNA based cancer theranostics—particularly for TNBC and Glioblastoma—and ultrasound responsive nanoplatforms for targeted drug delivery and immunotherapy.

Dr. Paulmurugan earned his Ph.D. in Molecular Virology from the University of Madras and completed postdoctoral training at UCLA and Stanford. He has published over 150 peer reviewed papers, holds multiple international patents, and is a recipient of the Distinguished Investigator Award from the Academy for Radiology & Biomedical Imaging Research.

Dr. Xiaowei Wang

Cardiovascular Scientist, Baker Heart and Diabetes Institute

Dr. Xiaowei Wang holds academic appointments at Monash University and the University of Melbourne. Her research integrates molecular imaging, vascular biology, and RNA based therapeutics to advance understanding and treatment of cardiovascular and cardiometabolic disease.

She leads a multidisciplinary program in Molecular Imaging and Nanotherapeutics, focusing on multimodality preclinical imaging (MRI, ultrasound, PET, microCT, fluorescence, and photoacoustic imaging), targeted drug and mRNA delivery systems, and translational strategies for atherosclerosis, thrombosis, and heart failure.

Dr. Wang earned her Ph.D. from Monash University, building on earlier clinical training as a cardiac technologist. She has received extensive recognition, including 14 Young Investigator Awards, 18 research prizes, and a National Heart Foundation Future Leader Fellowship, which includes the Paul Korner Innovation Award.

Dr. Abhijit De

Scientific Officer (G), ACTREC, Tata Memorial Centre

Dr. Abhijit De leads the Molecular Functional Imaging Laboratory. He previously held research appointments at Stanford University and UCLA, contributing to his international profile in molecular imaging and translational oncology.

His research focuses on developing molecular imaging probes, multimodality reporter systems, and targeted nanomedicine platforms to study tumor signaling, therapy resistance, and precision cancer therapeutics. His laboratory has filed and secured multiple patents, including a U.S. patent on BRET technology licensed from Stanford University.

Dr. De earned his Ph.D. from Banaras Hindu University and completed postdoctoral training at CCMB, UCLA, and Stanford. He has authored over 100 peer reviewed publications, holds an h index of 42, and has over 10,000 citations. He received the Best Teacher Award from the Tata Memorial Centre in 2025.

Dr. Kumaraswamy Thangaraj

Senior Principal Scientist, CCMB Hyderabad

Dr. Kumaraswamy Thangaraj is a leading Indian geneticist and molecular biologist. His research has significantly advanced the understanding of human genetic diversity, population history, and the molecular basis of inherited diseases across South Asia.

He leads a multidisciplinary research program focused on human evolutionary genetics, disease associated gene discovery, and the application of genomic tools to study population structure, migration, and health disparities. His work has provided major insights into mitochondrial DNA variation, Y chromosome phylogeny, and the genetic origins of diverse Indian populations.

Dr. Thangaraj earned his Ph.D. in Human Genetics from the University of Madras. He has authored over 200 peer reviewed publications, with more than 20,000 citations. His honors include the Padma Shri (2026), Rashtriya Vigyan Puraskar and the Shanti Swarup Bhatnagar Prize.

Dr. Rao V. L. Papineni

Adjunct Faculty, University of Kansas Medical Center

Dr. Rao V. L. Papineni is a distinguished scientist and innovator in the fields of nanotechnology, radiation oncology, and molecular imaging. He has held significant leadership roles in the biomedical industry, notably with Carestream Molecular Imaging.

He leads a pioneering research agenda focused on radiobiophotonics, the application of gold-based nanoparticles for radiotherapy enhancement, and the use of metabolic inhibitors like 2-Deoxy-D-glucose (2-DG) as radioprotectors and chemosensitizers. His multidisciplinary work encompasses the design of non-viral vectors for gene delivery, image-guided cell-based therapies, and the development of novel contrast agents.

Dr. Papineni has built a robust portfolio of intellectual property, holding several patents related to vibration-induced substance targeting and high-capacity delivery vectors. He has authored numerous peer-reviewed publications and has been a frequent speaker at international symposia.

Advisory Committee

Dr. Mikako Ogawa

Professor, Hokkaido University

Dr. Mikako Ogawa is a Professor at Hokkaido University’s Faculty of Pharmaceutical Sciences and a Principal Investigator at the Institute for Chemical Reaction Design and Discovery (ICReDD). She previously held a research appointment at the National Cancer Institute (NCI/NIH), contributing to her international profile in chemical biology and molecular imaging.

Her research focuses on developing innovative chemical probes and "smart" compounds to visualize and manipulate biomolecules using near-infrared light and radiation. Her laboratory is renowned for pioneering work in photoimmunotherapy and X-ray-triggered drug release, and she has secured multiple patents for her advancements in nanoparticle contrast agents.

Dr. Ogawa earned her Ph.D. in Pharmaceutical Sciences and has authored high-impact publications in journals such as Nature Medicine. Her contributions have been recognized with several honors, including the 2022 Mizushima Award from the Japan Society of Drug Delivery System.

Dr. Ichio Aoki

Senior Principal Investigator, QST Japan

Dr. Ichio Aoki is a Senior Principal Investigator at the Institute for Quantum Medical Science (iQMS), National Institutes for Quantum Science and Technology (QST), Japan. His research focuses on developing functional and molecular MRI techniques, specifically pioneering the use of manganese-enhanced MRI and theranostic nanoparticles to visualize biological processes and enable targeted drug delivery.

He earned his Ph.D. from the Meiji University of Oriental Medicine and completed his postdoctoral training at the National Institutes of Health (NIH) in the USA. Dr. Aoki has published extensively in the fields of molecular imaging and quantum medicine and serves as the President-elect of the Japanese Society for Molecular Imaging (JSMI).

Dr. Deepak Behera

President & CEO, Adaptive Research Inc

Dr. Deepak Behera is a nuclear medicine physician-scientist and the President & CEO of Adaptive Research Inc (USA). His research and clinical focus center on the advancement of molecular imaging technologies and radiopharmaceuticals, with significant contributions to early-phase clinical trials, medical affairs, and the launch of innovative radioligand therapies.

He earned his MD in Nuclear Medicine from the National Board of Examinations, India, and held a tenure at the Stanford University School of Medicine within the Molecular Imaging Program at Stanford (MIPS). Dr. Behera has published over 20 peer-reviewed articles and remains a key leader in the global molecular imaging community through his leadership roles in EPPIC Global and the Indo-American Society of Nuclear Medicine (IASNM).
