Call for Abstracts

FASMI–MISI Biennial Conference on Molecular Imaging  ·  Hyderabad, India  ·  October 2026

Advance the Science of
Molecular Imaging

We invite abstract submissions for the upcoming Biennial FASMI–MISI Conference on Molecular Imaging, bringing together Asian & global leaders across basic science, translational research, clinical practice, engineering, AI, and industry to showcase cutting-edge advances shaping the future of the field.

Researchers at all career stages, clinicians, engineers, and industry professionals are encouraged to submit original work. Selected abstracts will be considered for oral presentations, posters, and invited sessions.

Submission Tracks

Conference Themes & Focus Areas

Submit under the theme that best reflects your work — high-quality cross-disciplinary submissions are equally welcome.

Theme 1

Molecular Probes, Chemistry & Smart Contrast Agents

Activatable and bio-responsive probes, multimodal agents, bioorthogonal and click chemistry in vivo, fluorescence and bioluminescence imaging, optical clearing, and light-sheet microscopy.

Theme 2

Theranostics, Targeted Delivery & Nanoscale Systems

Radiotheranostics, molecular targeting, immune imaging, extracellular vesicles, DNA nanostructures, nanoparticle platforms, BBB penetration, and image-guided drug delivery.

Theme 3

Emerging Physics, Instrumentation & Imaging Modalities

Photoacoustic tomography, magnetic particle imaging, quantum sensing, advanced detectors, lensless microscopy, digital holography, and hybrid multimodal imaging systems.

Theme 4

AI-Enhanced & Computational Molecular Imaging

AI-driven reconstruction, processing and analysis; radiomics; multimodal data fusion; physics-informed learning; digital twins; and computational approaches for diagnosis and decision support.

Theme 5

Preclinical, Clinical & Translational Molecular Imaging

Small-animal imaging, clinical imaging across oncology, neurology, cardiology and immunology, imaging biomarkers, clinical trials, regulatory science, and imaging in drug development.

Theme 6

Futures, Innovation & Industry Perspectives

Next-generation imaging concepts, convergence with synthetic biology and digital health, commercialisation strategies, regulatory considerations, and industry–academia partnerships.

Open Topic Submissions Welcome

The conference welcomes high-quality abstracts beyond the six thematic areas — including novel imaging paradigms, unconventional applications, cross-disciplinary innovations, and emerging concepts that challenge current boundaries.

Researchers at all career stages, clinicians, engineers, and industry professionals are encouraged to submit original work. Selected abstracts will be considered for oral presentations, posters, and invited sessions.

Abstract Indexing & Publication

All accepted abstracts will be indexed and made available in Molecular Imaging and Biology (MIB), the official journal of the World Molecular Imaging Society (WMIS), ensuring broad visibility and long-term accessibility to the international molecular imaging community.
